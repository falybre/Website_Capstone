Additional Potential Panel Questions

Dataset & Data Processing Questions
How representative is your 2018-2020 dataset of current traffic patterns, especially considering post-pandemic changes in commuting patterns?
While our dataset predates significant pandemic-related changes, our model architecture can be retrained with newer data. The fundamental relationships between spatial and temporal factors remain relevant, and our approach can adapt to evolving traffic patterns with continuous model updates. Jan
Given the 17,300+ incidents in your dataset, did you perform any analysis on data quality or potential reporting biases?
Yes, we conducted extensive data quality analysis, including distribution tests across time periods to identify potential under-reporting during certain hours. We found approximately 3.8% missing values and implemented appropriate imputation strategies. We also employed cross-validation to ensure our model wasn't overfitting to any reporting biases. Vince
The concentration of incidents in Quezon City (50.37%) seems unusually high. Could this be due to data collection biases rather than actual incident rates?
We considered this possibility and cross-referenced with population density and road network complexity data. Quezon City's high percentage aligns with its status as the largest city in Metro Manila (covering about 25% of the metropolitan area), its complex road network, and high traffic volume. Nevertheless, we applied normalization techniques to account for potential reporting biases across regions. Jan
Did you consider external factors like special events, holidays, or weather conditions in your analysis?
Our current model includes temporal features like day of week and time of day, which capture some seasonal patterns. Weather data wasn't available in our primary dataset, which is a limitation we acknowledge. For future work, we recommend integrating weather APIs and special event calendars to enhance prediction accuracy, especially for anomalous traffic conditions. Miel
Methodology & Model Questions
How did you tune the hyperparameters for your XGBoost model, and which parameters had the most impact on performance?
We employed grid search with cross-validation to identify optimal hyperparameters. The most influential parameters were learning rate (eta=0.1), maximum depth of trees (max_depth=6), and number of boosting rounds (n_estimators=200). We found the model was particularly sensitive to the max_depth parameter, with deeper trees improving accuracy but requiring regularization to prevent overfitting. Rey
Your model achieved 92% accuracy, but what metrics did you use to evaluate imbalanced classes, and how did you address potential bias toward majority classes?
Beyond accuracy, we focused on precision (74%), recall (98%), and F1-score (85%) to better evaluate performance across all incident types. To address class imbalance, we implemented class weighting, used stratified sampling in cross-validation, and optimized for F1-score rather than raw accuracy to ensure balanced prediction quality across all incident categories. Rey
How did you validate your model's generalizability to unseen data and different traffic conditions?
We employed time-based cross-validation, training on earlier periods and testing on later ones to simulate real-world deployment. Additionally, we performed k-fold cross-validation with stratification to ensure consistent performance across different traffic patterns. Our test set included diverse temporal conditions (rush hours, weekends, holidays) to validate generalizability across varying traffic scenarios. Vince
What evaluation methodology did you use to compare XGBoost with Random Forest and Naive Bayes? Was the comparison fair?
We used identical training and testing datasets, feature engineering processes, and evaluation metrics for all models. Each model underwent hyperparameter optimization to ensure optimal performance. While XGBoost requires more hyperparameter tuning than Naive Bayes, we ensured a fair comparison by allocating appropriate optimization efforts to each algorithm's specific needs. Rey
Results & Interpretation Questions
What insights did you gain about the most significant factors contributing to traffic incidents in Metro Manila?
Our feature importance analysis revealed that temporal factors (time of day: 35%, day of week: 18%) and location (28%) were the most significant predictors. Specifically, rush hours (8:30 AM and 6:30 PM) showed 35% higher incident rates than other periods. The strong correlation (r=0.72) between day of week and incident patterns highlights how weekend traffic differs significantly from weekday patterns. Miel
How do you interpret the correlation (r=0.72) between day of week and weekends in your findings?
This strong positive correlation indicates that traffic incident patterns change significantly between weekdays and weekends. Weekdays show concentrated peaks during traditional rush hours, while weekends exhibit more evenly distributed incident patterns throughout the day. This suggests different driving behaviors and traffic management approaches are needed for weekends versus weekdays. Yuan
What specific insights did your spatial analysis provide about high-risk locations in Metro Manila?
Our spatial clustering revealed that EDSA, Quezon Avenue, and Commonwealth Avenue are the most incident-prone corridors. Specifically, we identified that intersections along these routes account for a disproportionate number of incidents. EDSA-Quezon Avenue intersection, for example, showed 180% higher incident rates than the average intersection. This suggests targeted infrastructure improvements at these specific locations could have outsized benefits. Miel
How did you rule out the possibility that your model is simply identifying general traffic volume rather than actual incident risk?
We incorporated traffic volume data as a control feature in our model, which allowed us to distinguish between high-traffic areas and high-risk areas. Some high-volume locations showed relatively low incident rates, while others showed disproportionately high rates. This normalization helps identify truly high-risk locations rather than just busy areas. Yuan
Implementation & Future Work Questions
How would your model handle sudden changes in traffic patterns due to unplanned events like political demonstrations or major sporting events?
Our current model would likely underperform during unexpected events. For real-world implementation, we recommend developing an anomaly detection system that identifies unusual patterns and triggers alternative prediction models. Integration with social media and event APIs would allow the system to anticipate non-routine traffic conditions and adjust predictions accordingly. Yuan
What specific technical infrastructure would be required to deploy your model in a production environment at the MMDA?
Implementation would require: (1) A dedicated server with GPU support for real-time processing, (2) API integration with existing MMDA traffic monitoring systems, (3) A data pipeline for continuous model updating and training, (4) A user-friendly dashboard for traffic controllers, and (5) Mobile-optimized alerts for field personnel. Cloud deployment would provide the necessary scalability and reliability. Rey
How would you address privacy concerns regarding location tracking if your system were to be implemented citywide?
Our approach uses aggregated data rather than individual vehicle tracking. We recommend implementing data anonymization protocols, adhering to data privacy regulations, obtaining appropriate permits, and being transparent about data usage. We can achieve accurate predictions using anonymized, aggregated data without compromising individual privacy. Justine
What are the limitations of your current model and what specific improvements would you recommend for future work?
Current limitations include: (1) Fixed temporal patterns that don't adapt to evolving traffic behaviors, (2) Limited weather and special event data integration, and (3) Lack of real-time updating capability. Future improvements should include: developing an adaptive online learning approach, integrating multiple data sources (weather, events, road work), implementing a reinforcement learning component to continuously improve predictions, and creating a city-specific ensemble of models. Yuan
